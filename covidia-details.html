Covidia (Covid diagnose application)

Project information

  • Category: Flutter App
  • Developer Team: Smart Health Research Mataram University
  • Project Timeline: Jul 2022 - Des 2022

App Description

Covidia (Covid diagnose application) is a mobile application built to simplify the identification process covid 19 patients by analyzing the results of x-rays image of the patient's lungs using machine learning.

Problems

The global impact of Covid-19, caused by the acute respiratory syndrome coronavirus 2 (Sars-CoV-2), is evident in the data from Worldometers as of July 14, 2021, with 188,563,150 confirmed cases, 4,065,129 fatalities, and 172,396,201 recoveries. While governments offer various testing methods, such as Rapid Antibody, Antigen Swab, and PCR tests, there's a pressing need to enhance accuracy, reduce testing time, and lower costs. Current studies reveal that X-ray and CT scans provide more accurate results, with CT scans detecting 97% of Covid-19 cases. However, the expensive setup costs for CT scans pose a challenge. On the other hand, the use of X-ray imagery, employing Convolutional Neural Networks (CNN), achieves a remarkable 98% accuracy, positioning it as a potential alternative for diagnosing Covid-19 patients. Given these challenges, there's an urgent need to innovate testing methods by integrating machine learning and applications, aiming to improve overall testing efficiency and accessibility.

Process

I served as Mobile Developer and UI/UX designer

In my dual role as a mobile developer and UI/UX designer, I played a pivotal role in an interdisciplinary effort to improve Covid-19 diagnostics. Conducting comprehensive research on radiology experts' practices, I gained insights into their patient identification methods and addressed existing challenges. Leveraging this understanding, I designed a user experience and interface tailored to their specific needs. Applying Flutter and Dart, I seamlessly integrated machine learning into the project to enhance diagnostic capabilities. Thorough testing, encompassing both manual and automation processes, ensured the application's reliability. Collaborating closely with a cross-functional team, including ML engineers and experts, the project aimed to provide a valuable solution for the field of radiology and Covid-19 diagnosis, blending technical expertise with medical insights.

Tools and Technology

Flutter

Flutter is utilized to build the mobile application. I chose Flutter because of its ability to create multi-platform applications with a single code base, and I selected it due to my proficiency in the framework.

Firebase

Firebase is employed to store application data, including diagnosis information and X-ray images.

Tensorflow Lite

TensorFlow Lite functions as the library I utilized to seamlessly integrate machine learning into the application, facilitating the invocation of predictions.

Github

GitHub is used for version control and collaborate with my teammates.

The Challenges

One of the primary challenges in this project involved the integration of a machine learning model into the mobile system using TensorFlow Lite. This task required a deep dive into understanding the intricacies of TensorFlow Lite and determining the most effective way to seamlessly incorporate the machine learning capabilities into the mobile application. Additionally, collaboration with experts in the field was crucial for the success of this project. Working alongside domain specialists introduced challenges related to effective communication, ensuring alignment between the machine learning model's requirements and the overall objectives of the mobile system. This collaborative effort demanded a keen focus on clear communication and understanding to bridge the gap between technical intricacies and project goals.
