Jalur: Faith Assistance

Project information

  • Category: iOS Native App
  • Developer Team: HTM
  • Project Timeline: Sep 2023 - Dec 2023

App Description

Jalur is an app that assist occupational drivers in practicing their faith by identifying and suggesting mosques along the route based on their location and local prayer times.

In December of 2023, Jalur was selected as the most impactful application and became the representative of Apple Developer Academy @Infinite Learning Batam

Problems

Occupational drivers often work long hours, sometimes up to 20 hours a day, while traveling across the country. They face a pressing challenge in identifying nearby mosques along their routes and aligning with the respective prayer times. Compounded by their unfamiliarity with the areas they traverse and the varying prayer schedules across Indonesia, some drivers resort to praying on the sidewalk. This underscores the critical need for a solution that provides them with information about the nearest mosques along their journey, including accurate prayer timings, ensuring they can fulfill their religious obligations more seamlessly.

v0.1.0

In the initial version of Jalur, based on our research and interviews with drivers in Indonesia, we discovered that many face difficulty finding suitable rest areas while driving, leading to risks of fatigue and microsleep. Moreover, irregular drivers often pass by gas stations, rest areas, or other rest stops without realizing it due to unfamiliar routes. They assume there is another gas station ahead, but in reality, there might not be, compelling them to continue the journey without a break, increasing the risk of accidents.

v0.2.0

In this version, we refined our focus by delving deeper into the issues identified during testing and feedback collection. Through analysis, we uncovered specific edge cases prevalent in Indonesia, particularly concerning occupational drivers who work long hours on the road. These individuals face the challenge of finding suitable places for their daily five prayers. Given that they travel to different regions every day, each with its unique prayer times, they encounter difficulty estimating the locations of mosques for their prayers. Consequently, they are sometimes compelled to pray on sidewalks or end up missing their prayer times.

This exploration and understanding of the nuanced challenges led us to a more detailed comprehension of the specific needs of occupational drivers, informing further refinements in our solution.

v0.3.0

In this version, we aimed to simplify the design while introducing valuable enhancements. We incorporated parking area data into the information and recommendation algorithm for mosque searches. This addition was prompted by feedback indicating that occupational drivers, such as logistics and truck drivers, often navigate large vehicles and require parking spaces. Many of them abandon the intention to pray at a mosque due to inadequate parking facilities. Subsequently, we conducted AB testing and user testing to gather feedback for the next iteration. This iterative approach ensures that our solution aligns more closely with the practical needs and preferences of our users.

v1.0.0

In the final version of Jalur, we focused on refining the user experience to be more straightforward and user-friendly. Additionally, we introduced animations and illustrations to enhance the application's user interface. We optimized the mosque search algorithm along the travel route and incorporated new features such as trip scheduling and history. These updates were based on valuable insights gathered from previous user testing, ensuring that the application aligns seamlessly with user preferences and delivers a more engaging and efficient experience.

The Challenges

Implementing MapKit and CoreLocation into our application posed a significant challenge for both myself and the team. Prior to this project, none of us had hands-on experience in integrating mapping services, making it a learning curve that required dedicated effort and collaboration. Overcoming this obstacle marked a valuable milestone in our journey.

Ensuring Jalur could adeptly handle scenarios like route changes, delays, and early arrivals at the destination mosque required crafting intricate algorithms. This task, though challenging, proved to be a rewarding experience that honed my technical and user experience skills.

Jalur, my debut app on the App Store, faced its share of hurdles during the submission process, leading to an initial rejection. Collaborating with the team, I successfully addressed the issues raised during the review, underlining our commitment to delivering a high-quality product.
